Ethiopia's Medieval Camelot

Discover Ethiopia's "Camelot of Africa" on this 3-day journey to medieval Gondar. This magnificent city served as Ethiopia's capital from the 17th to 19th centuries, earning its romantic nickname from the spectacular royal castles and palaces that still stand today as testaments to a golden age of Ethiopian architecture and culture.

Explore the magnificent 17th-century royal castles and palaces of Emperor Fasilides and his successors. The UNESCO World Heritage royal enclosure contains a remarkable collection of buildings, each with its own unique character and architectural style. Wander through the grand halls, climb ancient staircases, and imagine the courtly life that once flourished within these stone walls.

Visit ornate churches like Debre Berhan Selassie, famous for its stunning ceiling paintings featuring dozens of cherubic angels that seem to watch over visitors with benevolent gazes. Experience the unique blend of Ethiopian and European architectural influences that make Gondar's buildings so distinctive. Learn about Ethiopia's medieval golden age, when the city was a center of learning, art, and international diplomacy, connecting Ethiopia with the wider world through trade and diplomatic relations.
